# transport via playlist: build a one-item video playlist, then play / pause /
# continue / stop it. Same transport as transport-playeropen.txt, but the video
# is started from the video playlist (id 1) rather than opened directly.
# The single item is one video file: a Family Guy episode.
# Format: '# title', then a raw JSON-RPC request line, then '[state] observation'.

# clear the video playlist
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":1,"method":"Playlist.Clear","params":{"playlistid":1}}
[stopped] nothing playing yet; the video playlist is emptied

# add one Family Guy episode (a single video file) to the playlist
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":2,"method":"Playlist.Add","params":{"playlistid":1,"item":{"file":"/home/pipas/net/storage03/Serials/Family Guy/Family Guy Blue Harvest 2007 DVDRip [A Release-Lounge H264].mp4"}}}
[stopped] still nothing playing; the episode is now queued in the playlist

# start the playlist
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":3,"method":"Player.Open","params":{"item":{"playlistid":1}}}
[playing] Family Guy (Blue Harvest) starts playing fullscreen

# press pause
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":4,"method":"Input.ExecuteAction","params":{"action":"pause"}}
[paused] playback freezes; the OSD shows the pause indicator

# press play (continue)
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":5,"method":"Input.ExecuteAction","params":{"action":"play"}}
[playing] playback resumes from where it was paused

# press stop
{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":6,"method":"Input.ExecuteAction","params":{"action":"stop"}}
[stopped] video stops; Kodi returns to the menu/home
